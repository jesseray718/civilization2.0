## What Is This?

The AeroCement Ecosystem is a unified open-source infrastructure system demonstrating that the fundamental crises of **energy, shelter, and food** can be solved simultaneously using passive thermodynamic systems, volumetric porous materials, and biointensive permaculture — all constructible from **concrete, dirt, water, and scrap** at **zero recurring energy cost**.

## Core Innovation: The Passive Solar-Thermal Loop

A single passive loop per m² of collector delivers:

| Output | Power | Replaces |
|--------|-------|----------|
| **Mechanical Work** | 76.6 W (Stirling engine, belt drive) | Diesel generator / grid motor |
| **Thermal Cooling** | 854.4 W (Cold Battery at 2°C) | Electric A/C / chiller |
| **Thermal Heating** | 854.4 W (geothermal mass storage) | Gas furnace / heat pump |

- **Solar conversion efficiency:** 93.1%
- **Passive COP (transport):** 8,544
- **Parasitic electrical input:** 0.1 W (friction only)
- **Grid electricity required:** 0 W

**How it works:** Activated carbon open-cell aerocement absorbs 98% of solar irradiance volumetrically. Air flows THROUGH the porous material, not over it. Heated air rises via stack effect (gravity-driven, zero electricity). Hot dry air descends into a subterranean wet porous labyrinth at 10ft depth (55°F steady state). Evaporative + sensible cooling produces 2°C output. A Stirling engine extracts mechanical work from the temperature differential. No pumps. No fans. No grid.

## Three Pillars

### 🔥 Energy — Passive Solar-Thermal Loop
Volumetric blackbody absorption → stack-effect transport → subterranean evaporative cooling → Stirling work extraction → Cold Battery storage. Zero fuel. Zero emissions. Zero operating cost.

### 🏠 Shelter — Geodesic AeroCement Domes
Acetone-silicone treated waste cardboard cores skinned with aerocement and GFRC. Click-flange assembly. Scales from 1V emergency shelters (4-hour deploy) to 50V stadiums (30,000+ sq ft). Breathing wall facades purify urban air.

### 🌱 Life — Biointensive Permaculture Guilds
Black Locust keystone trees (nitrogen fixation + rot-resistant timber + honey). Vertical quail towers (50-80 birds, 2×2 ft footprint). Closed-loop ferrocement aquaponics (algae → tilapia → duckweed → worms → soil). Heirloom seed banks. No GMO. No proprietary seed.
